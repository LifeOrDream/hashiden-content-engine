# Blueprint 05 — The Underdog + the Fairness/Trust beat (~50s)

The emotional opposite of the superpower videos. Sells the credibility angle (fair launch / no pre-mine / dynamic re-opening board) as HOPE, not a tokenomics chart. Most "root for the little guy" shareable energy. Raja carries it with one flicker of real nerves.

## HOOK
Raja, grinning, cricket bat slung like a weapon, straight to camera: lists the giants' advantages (USA money, China numbers, "North Korea has… whatever that is" — quick muted cut to Marshal mid-rant), then welcomes the zero-percent odds.

## GROUNDING TO WEAVE (as a battle cry, not a fairness FAQ)
- No pre-sale, no insider bags, no "team allocation" — the only way in is to mine.
- The board wipes clean every 4 hours; emissions shift; the game re-opens → a small hungry community can flip a superpower "before lunch."

## BEATS
1. [hook] Raja lists the giants, jabs at NK (muted cut), grins at being given no chance. INTENT: Raja recruits the overlooked by making underdog status a flex.
2. [setup] He walks the mining floor, tapping beasts from a dozen small nations who light up behind him. The "secret they hate": no pre-sale, no insiders, no team bags — the only way in is the pickaxe. INTENT: Raja arms the little guys with the fairness fact, as ammunition.
3. [escalation] The 4-hour clock resets behind him with a satisfying CHUNK; the board re-opens; a loud-hearted small country can dethrone an empire before lunch. INTENT: Raja sells the opening — the math that favors hunger over size.
4. [cliffhanger] He winks, turns to leave — "see you on the leaderboard, we'll be the ones in first" — then a half-notch flicker of real doubt as he clocks the timer. INTENT: Raja covers nerves with bravado (the human crack that makes us believe him).
5. [end_card] 03:00:00 + CTA.
