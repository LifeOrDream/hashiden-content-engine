# Blueprint 03 — The Axis Schemes (comic-villain, ~60s)

Reveals the strategic depth of the game (the 4-hour breathing cycle = a weapon) through a scheming-villains scene with comedy. The meta button: they catch us watching.

## HOOK
Pitch-black bunker, one swinging red light. A heavy paw SLAMS a steel table — Marshal declares the Americans think they've already won.

## GROUNDING TO WEAVE (as a plan, not a tutorial)
- The emissions breathe every 4 hours → the late-cycle steal ("let them mine first, take it in the final hour") teaches the mechanic AS strategy.

## BEATS
1. [hook] Marshal slams the table: the Americans think they've already won. INTENT: Marshal rallies the room (and overstates, as always).
2. [setup] Reveal the trio around a holographic globe pulsing with the 4-hour cycle: Master Long serene (jade pickaxe), Volkov a statue, Marshal pacing. Long says let them mine first, let them celebrate. INTENT: Long teaches patience as a weapon — to the room and the viewer.
3. [escalation] Long traces the breathing emissions curve; "in the final hour, when they are fat and slow—" / Volkov completes it: "—we take everything." INTENT: the villains make the mechanic feel like a trap snapping shut.
4. [comic turn] Marshal erupts in superlatives (greatest mining victory in the history of all nations, living and dead). Long, dry, not looking up: "He says that every cycle." Volkov: "Every cycle." INTENT: deflate Marshal for the laugh; humanize the villains.
5. [cliffhanger / meta] A red REC dot blinks. All three slowly turn to camera. Long: "...how long has that been recording?" Volkov's paw reaches for the lens — SMASH to black, glass-crack. INTENT: the fourth-wall scare implicates the viewer.
6. [end_card] 12:00:00 + CTA.

## PLACEHOLDER SEED LINES (replace / keep in-character)
- Keep Long's lines proverb-shaped and few; Volkov minimal; Marshal grandiose.
