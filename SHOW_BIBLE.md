# HASHIDEN — Series Bible

The layer above WORLD_BIBLE.md. The world bible says who is in the show;
this says what the show is FOR. Every writers-room pass, chapter title,
season arc, and trailer blueprint answers to this document.

## The premise

Humanity is starting to climb the Kardashev scale — measuring itself, for
the first time, by how much power it can harness rather than how much
territory it can hold. HASHIDEN is the story of that climb told from the
bottom: twelve nations of dog-warriors mining the value that funds the
ascent. The rockets get the headlines. The dogs dig the fuel.

## The mission (why we make it)

Make people imagine what's possible. Pull attention above earth-level
conflict toward a species-level story: humanity together, reaching for
the stars — the way Star Wars (1977) gave a cynical decade a mythic,
optimistic frame without pretending conflict doesn't exist. We keep the
full dramatic palette — greed, exploitation, real science, underdogs,
secrets, thrillers, powers — but the show's moral center is ascent:
every arc bends toward "we go up."

## The Star Wars operating manual, translated

1977 Star Wars worked because of four things; each has our analog:
1. Mythic structure (hero's journey) -> every beast's wiki arc: Pup to
   Ascended, epithets, technique debuts, prestiges.
2. A lived-in universe (used future) -> the mines, the scuffed gear, the
   war economy. Nothing chrome; everything earned.
3. A technology leap that made it filmable (ILM) -> the AI content
   engine. Generation cost collapse is our motion control rig.
4. Arriving exactly when the culture needed hope -> the SpaceX/IPO/
   Kardashev moment. The audience is already looking up.
And the part Star Wars never had: the audience OWNS the cast and writes
itself in (Direct-Your-Beast), and the show never stops — a chapter
every 4 hours, 42 per weekly season, 24/7, produced by gameplay.

## The fourth wall is the scale itself

Kardashev measures civilizations by energy harnessed. Hashiden's
production is funded by compute bought with the game's own fees. So the
show's budget is its plot: the more the war is played, the more compute
burns, the richer the show renders (still -> gif -> clip -> cinematic),
the higher the civilization-of-the-game literally climbs. The compute
ledger in every chapter is the series' Kardashev meter. No other show
can say its audience IS the ascent.

## Thematic spine per season

Seasons are named as decimal climbs of the scale (Season 1: "We Don't
Register" / toward the first microsol...). Each season: the 12 countries
compete to fund the next rung; the finale settles who leads the climb;
the cliffhanger seeds the next constraint (mass-to-orbit, power, chips,
the moon — the real engineering ladder, dramatized). Real science
approaches stay real: reusability, solar, radiators, mass drivers —
parodied warmly, never dumbly.

## Tonal contract

- Optimism is the spine; conflict is the muscle. Villains are greed and
  smallness, not nations. Every country is a key player.
- Deadpan over bombast. The gum pop beats the speech.
- Real numbers when we can cite them; wonder when we can't.
- On-chain is canon; owner lore is apocrypha; the citation discipline
  (cycle/clip/tx) is the show's trust grammar.
